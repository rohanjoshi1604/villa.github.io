Dosa, masala itli

There’s a famous saying that goes, “When everything else fails, there’s always dosa”. Alright, we just made that up but if there’s one thing that everyone agrees upon, it’s that South Indian food is glorious. Sure, it tends to get stereotyped and reduced to the mere mention of dosa, idli and medhu wada. But it goes without saying that our folks down south do a great job at making these delicacies. Fortunately for us, we don’t have to travel too far to get our fix because some of these places.

Pasta

A white pebbled courtyard and a canopy of a picturesque banyan tree welcome guests to this mansion turned al fresco eatery. Here, chef Dhruv Oberoi whips up delightful European and Mediterranean dishes using the freshest seasonal produce. Their house-made pastas feature interesting shapes that are not easily available off the shelf. We highly recommend Olive Bar & Kitchen’s Aglio e’ Olio cooked in garlic-chilli, olive oil and parmesan cheese, Tortellini stuffed with goat cheese in a brown butter sauce with leafy greens, and Alle Genovese cooked in pesto sauce with seasonal beans.
